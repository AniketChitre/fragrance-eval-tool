Fragrance Bloom Intensity Evaluation Protocol – Shampoo

Date Issued: 5 Dec 2025
Owner: UFH
Location: Port Sunlight Research Lab

1. OBJECTIVE
Evaluate fragrance intensity and bloom (room-fill) performance of raw materials and finished fragrances in a standardised shampoo base. This protocol supports comparative assessment, scale-based evaluation, and determination of acceptable bloom levels.

2. SCOPE
Applies to internal trained sensory panels conducted in fragrance evaluation booths. Covers sample preparation, environmental setup, panel procedure, and data collection and analysis.

3. PANEL SETUP

Panel details:
- Panel type: Trained internal sensory panel
- Panel size: Minimum 10 panellists per assessment
- Replicates: 3 per sample
- Training: Passed olfactory screening and calibrated to scale anchors
- Demographics: Mixed gender and age preferred

Booth configuration (Port Sunlight reference):
- Height: 2600 mm
- Length: 1630 mm
- Width: 980 mm
- Shower head at far end of booth
- Distance from shower head to sink bottom: 57 cm

4. EVALUATION CONDITIONS (SHAMPOO)

Environmental:
- Location: Fragrance booth
- Ambient temperature: 20–25 °C
- Humidity: Not controlled; ~90% RH observed during shower use

Water and equipment:
- Water temperature: 35–40 °C
- Flow rate: 4 L/min
- Beaker: 1 litre
- Sink/basin approx. dimensions:
  - Depth: 300 mm
  - Length: 300 mm
  - Width: 400 mm

Product application:
- Product dose: 10 g shampoo (calibration sample may differ)
- Shower running time: 1 minute
- Incubation: 1 minute post-application
- Evaluation distance: ~1630 mm (booth length)

5. EVALUATION SCALE – BLOOM INTENSITY

Scale definition:
- Linear scale: 0–10
- Increment: 0.5
- 0 = No fragrance
- 10 = Extremely strong fragrance

Anchoring:
- Reference: Intensity 5
- Low control: 3
- High control: 8
- Controls provided:
  - Full fragrance: 3, 5, 8
  - Raw materials: 3 and 8

6. EVALUATION PROCESS – SHAMPOO

Materials required:
- Shampoo sample (10 g ≈ 10 ml)
- Warm water (20 g at 35–40 °C)
- 1 L beaker
- Timer
- Disposable gloves

Sample preparation:
1. Mix 10 g shampoo with 20 g warm water in beaker
2. Agitate to create foam (whisking motion)
3. Cover with foil to prevent early fragrance release
4. Equilibrate in evaluation room for 15 minutes
5. Close booth door securely
6. Run shower at 37 °C for 3 minutes to set humidity
7. Label booth with randomised code

Lathering procedure:
1. Remove foil from beaker
2. Plug sink
3. Place beaker under shower head
4. Run shower at 37 °C directly onto sample for 1 minute

Bloom assessment:
1. Wait 1 minute for booth saturation
2. Panellist evaluates through booth window/opening
3. Record bloom intensity using 0–10 scale

7. OPERATIONAL NOTES
- Minimum 1 hour ventilation between assessments in same booth
- Use fresh gloves and clean bowl for each sample
- Decontaminate with unfragranced antibacterial spray
- Maintain consistent timing, water volume, and temperature
- Panellists must not wear personal fragrance or fragranced products

8. PANEL DESIGN GUIDANCE
- Base formulation: Identical for all samples
- Raw material dosage: Fixed
- Labelling: Randomised 3-digit codes
- Presentation order: Fully randomised per panellist

9. DATA COLLECTION AND ANALYSIS

Data capture:
- Tool: Compusense or equivalent
- Data: Intensity scores, bloom scores, rankings, liking scores

Statistical analysis:
- ANOVA (1-way or 2-factor)
- Tukey HSD
- Friedman test
- Binomial test
- Significance threshold: p < 0.05

10. FRAGRANCE INTENSITY SCALE (REFERENCE)

0  - No detectable fragrance
1  - Extremely weak; barely perceptible
2  - Very weak; faint
3  - Weak; noticeable, no presence
4  - Mild; limited diffusion
5  - Moderate; balanced
6  - Distinct; fills immediate area
7  - Strong; fills the space
8  - Very strong; dominates room
9  - Extremely strong; overpowering
10 - Maximum intensity; saturating
